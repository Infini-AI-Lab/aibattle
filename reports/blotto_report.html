How Colonel Blotto works

A two-player game of simultaneous resource allocation, played over 20 rounds. Each round:
  • Both players secretly split 100 troops across 5 battlefields worth 1, 2, 3, 4 and 5 points (15 points up for grabs per round).
  • Allocations are revealed at the same time — neither sees the other's before committing.
  • On each battlefield the larger force wins that field's points; an exact tie awards the field to neither.
  • Whoever takes the most points wins the round; only resolved past rounds are shown to the opponent.
After 20 rounds the higher cumulative score wins (equal totals draw). No hidden cards or chance — the difficulty is purely strategic: spread thin to grab the cheap fields, or stack up to guarantee the expensive ones, while reading and misdirecting the opponent across rounds.
What the model sees each round: the 5 battlefields and their point values, its 100-troop budget, and the results of past rounds — not the opponent's current allocation (both commit at the same time).
